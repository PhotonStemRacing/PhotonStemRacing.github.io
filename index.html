Our Story

What Is F1 in Schools?

F1 in Schools challenges secondary school students to work as a real engineering and enterprise company: designing, analysing, manufacturing and racing a miniature Formula 1 car powered by a CO2 cartridge, then pitching that work to judges exactly as a professional motorsport team would.

Team History

We began in 2023 as Cadet team Kevlar, placing 2nd overall in the state and 4th in racing. In 2024, having renamed ourselves Aramid after realising "Kevlar" was a trademarked name, we placed 1st in racing and 4th overall, with our only racing loss coming against Team Bluefin. Our car set a state record, the fastest in state history, with a lap time of 1.26 seconds over 20 metres — though a weak enterprise campaign held us to 4th overall for development. Shame got the better of us in 2025, and we didn't compete. This year, 2026, we're back and racing in the Professional Class. Our enterprise team has been fully reworked, featuring Evan Goodwin, a master of persuasive writing, Lizzie Cooke, a master of graphic design, and Zeke Short, a master of all things Formula 1. Our engineering team remains unchanged: Gifford Roy, a master of engineering and CAD, and Bruno Gomez Pena, a master of manufacturing and car painting.
